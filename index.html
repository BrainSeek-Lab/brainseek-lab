Focus Areas

What We Work On

Our research spans the breadth of modern deep learning — algorithmic efficiency, robustness, brain-inspired computing, and efficient language models.

🛡️
Adversarial Attacks & Defence
Studying how deep learning models fail under adversarial perturbations and building robust defences — from certified defences to empirical hardening of vision and language models.
📦
Model Compression & Quantization
Making neural networks smaller and faster without sacrificing accuracy. We work on pruning, quantization-aware training, and hardware-aware compression for efficient deployment.
🎓
Knowledge Distillation
Transferring the knowledge of large teacher models into compact student networks — enabling capable models to run on resource-constrained devices.
🗣️
Small Language Models
Designing efficient SLMs (including ShisuLM) for constrained environments. We explore architecture choices, training strategies, and distillation techniques for capable small-scale language models.
⚡
Spiking Neural Networks
Designing and training SNNs for energy-efficient inference — including ANN-SNN conversion (PASCAL), direct training, and biologically plausible learning rules like Equilibrium Propagation.
🔬
Neuromorphic Hardware
Building DL accelerators tailored for sparse, spike-based computation. We exploit dual sparsity (APex) and design quantization schemes for real-time neuromorphic deployment.
🌀
Energy Based Models
Exploring energy-based learning frameworks as principled alternatives to standard supervised training — including Equilibrium Propagation as a biologically plausible credit assignment mechanism.

PASCAL: Precise and Efficient ANN-SNN Conversion using Spike Accumulation and Adaptive Layerwise Activation
A mathematically grounded method to convert ANNs to SNNs with minimal inference timesteps. ResNet-34 achieves ~74% ImageNet accuracy with a 56× reduction in timesteps over prior methods.
