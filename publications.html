Transactions on ML Research (TMLR) ANN-SNN Conversion SNN
PASCAL: Precise and Efficient ANN-SNN Conversion using Spike Accumulation and Adaptive Layerwise Activation
SNNs offer energy efficiency over ANNs by replacing multiply-accumulate ops with sparse accumulate ops. PASCAL achieves a mathematically exact ANN-SNN equivalence using a novel spike accumulation scheme and adaptive per-layer quantization of the QCFS activation. ResNet-34 reaches ~74% ImageNet accuracy with a 56× reduction in inference timesteps versus prior methods.
Pranav Ramesh, Gopalakrishnan Srinivasan

IEEE ISVLSI Hardware Accelerator SNN
APEX: A Dual-Sparsity Accelerator for Precise and Efficient SNN Inference
APex is a hardware accelerator design that simultaneously exploits both spatial sparsity (zero activations within a timestep) and temporal sparsity (silent neurons across timesteps) in SNNs, enabling significant energy and compute savings on neuromorphic hardware. Published at IEEE International Symposium on VLSI (ISVLSI).
BrainSeek Lab, IIT Madras

Conference Bio-Inspired Learning
Scaling Equilibrium Propagation to Deeper Networks
Equilibrium Propagation (EP) is a biologically plausible learning rule that eliminates the need for explicit error backpropagation. This work addresses the key challenge of making EP practical on deeper architectures, narrowing the gap with standard gradient-based training on complex vision benchmarks.
Sankar Vinayak, Gopalakrishnan Srinivasan
